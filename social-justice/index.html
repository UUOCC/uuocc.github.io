Social Justice at UUOCC

UUOCC is a small congregation, but we take our commitment to social justice seriously. Our shared values – as Unitarian Universalists – plainly defines why we take action for social and environmental justice. We contribute financially to UUFaithActionNJ, the statewide UU social action organization. We participate in workshops and activities that they sponsor, particularly in the areas of environmental justice, climate action and immigration policy. (For more information visit www.uufaithaction.org)

Within Ocean County we collectively and individually support a wide variety of organizations. We offer assistance where we feel our small community can make the most impact. When it’s feasible, we partner with other organizations and faith communities to multiply results. We’ll speak to community leaders and make informed decisions. Individually, we do what we can. Together, we can do great things.

UUOCC has been recognized as a UU Welcoming Community and Stands on the Side of Love.

We stand against intolerance and hate

The UUOCC chose a primary issue for which we could make positive change in 2025. The cause of the unhoused in Ocean County was democratically chosen by the congregation. Since that vote, we have taken 2nd and/or special collections to benefit The Hope Center, Interfaith Hospitality Network and Just Believe Inc. We are scheduled to help more organizations through the remainder of the year including Fulfill of Monmouth & Ocean County. We’ve heard from several community leaders on the topic including Paul Hulse and Rev. Steve Brigham. We are scheduled to hear from Stephanie Ferrier of Fulfill, in October, 2025.

The UUOCC continues to follow closely the fight to allow Christ Episcopal Church of Toms River to open a 17-bed overnight shelter for the homeless at their location. We’ve shown up for the ‘Voice for the Voiceless’ rally, at council and zoning commission meetings and an interfaith prayer service. We are exploring support and volunteer opportunities with other exceptional groups like the Toms River Housing & Homeless Coalition.

We have one Social Justice Team, but individually we may have our own justice priorities. For instance, UUOCC is a supporter of the Toms River Pride Parade. We continue to make our voices heard in protecting against devastating cuts to programs like Medicaid and Medicare. We show up and call for immigration justice, racial justice and reproductive justice.

Add a nod of support. Or, add your unique voice and energy to our purpose. Join us in making change.
